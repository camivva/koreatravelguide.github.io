K-ETA



Foreign visitors who want to enter the Republic of Korea, need to apply for a K-ETA prior to their trip. K-ETA (Korean Electronic Travel Authorization) is an online electronic travel authorization that allows foreigners to visit South Korea for an estimated period of time.

Things to know before applying for K-ETA.

  • You should apply for K-ETA with enough time. It is recommended to apply for K-ETA 72 hours before departure day.
  • The K-ETA application can be approved or denied.
  • The cost of K-ETA is around 9-10 USD with an additional fee included. There is no refund when the application is denied.
  • K-ETA is not a visa. There is no guarantee of entry into the country, therefore, the Korean Immigration Service Officer will have the final decision.

** Note **

From April 1, 2023, to December 31, 2024, K-ETA will be temporarily exempted in 22 countries. If you have a valid passport from the list of countries eligible for exemption, it is not necessary to apply for K-ETA. However, it is recommended that you do so as you have the benefit of applying at no cost and can skip the entry form. To find out if you are eligible, visit the official website of Visit Korea.

Public Health Requirements



Visitors do not need to present a COVID-19 vaccine certificate or a pre-departure COVID-19 test. However, it is mandatory for visitors to register and report their health condition prior to their trip using the Q-code system. Q-code is a QR code that contains all the personal information and vaccination information needed to enter the country. Registration is completely free of charge!

What documents do I need to have my Q-code?

  • Valid passport
  • Airline ticket
  • Vaccination records

Travel Insurance



South Korea is a peaceful and safe country to visit, you can go anywhere without any worries, however, you may encounter obstacles. It is not mandatory to have travel insurance to enter Korea. Nevertheless, it is highly recommended to have one as you do not know what obstacles may occur.
